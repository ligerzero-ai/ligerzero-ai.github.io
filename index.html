Han Lin Mai

Postdoctoral Researcher | Computational Materials Scientist

I am a Computational Materials Scientist currently working as a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Sustainable Materials GmbH in Dusseldorf, Germany. My research focus is on computational metallurgy, specifically workflow digitalisation and grain boundary segregation.

I hold a PhD in Engineering (Materials Science) from The University of Sydney, where my thesis explored grain boundary segregation and cohesion in engineering alloys using first-principles calculations.

I am also a core developer for pyiron, a Python-based workflow manager for computational materials science.
