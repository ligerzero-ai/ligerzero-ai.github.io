Quantifying changes to solute segregation behaviour at interfaces in additively manufactured Ti-6Al-4V

Breen, A.J., Davids, W.J., Chen, H., Mai, H.L., Nomoto, K., Cui, X., Liao, X., Primig, S. and Ringer, S.P.

Acta Materialia, p. 121904 (2026)

View on Google Scholar →
Key Points
  • Combines correlative transmission Kikuchi diffraction and atom probe tomography with first-principles DFT calculations to quantify Gibbsian interfacial excess at alpha/alpha grain boundaries in electron powder bed fusion Ti-6Al-4V
  • DFT calculations on model grain boundaries confirmed the thermodynamic driving force for V and Fe enrichment and Al depletion at boundaries
  • V segregation generally increased with boundary misorientation angle
  • Good agreement between experimental APT observations and DFT predictions highlights the influence of local defect structures on segregation behaviour

A high-throughput ab initio study of elemental segregation and cohesion at ferritic-iron grain boundaries

Mai, H.L., Cui, X.Y., Hickel, T., Neugebauer, J. and Ringer, S.

Acta Materialia, 121288 (2025)

View on Google Scholar →
Key Points
  • Comprehensive DFT evaluation of segregation energies and cohesive effects for all elements from H to U (Z = 1-92) across substitutional sites in six model ferritic iron grain boundaries
  • Cohesive effects assessed using both DDEC6 bond-order analysis and modified Rice-Thomson-Wang theory of interfacial cohesion
  • Segregant effects on GB cohesion vary drastically as a function of site character; accurate predictions require thermodynamic averaging across energy distributions
  • All data publicly available on GitHub for community use

A first-principles study of P segregation and cohesion at iron grain boundaries in the presence of transition metals

Mai, H.L., Cui, X.Y., Scheiber, D., Romaner, L. and Ringer, S.P.

Acta Materialia, 250, 118850 (2023)

View on Google Scholar →
Key Points
  • DFT and kinetic simulations of P co-segregation with ten transition metals (Co, Cr, Cu, Mn, Mo, Ni, Nb, Ti, V, W) at ferritic iron grain boundaries
  • Phosphorus alone is unlikely to cause intergranular fracture through weakened interfacial bonding
  • The interplay between P and TM co-segregation governs embrittlement behaviour, challenging the conventional view that P is the sole driver of temper embrittlement in steels

The segregation of transition metals to iron grain boundaries and their effects on cohesion

Mai, H.L., Cui, X.Y., Scheiber, D., Romaner, L. and Ringer, S.P.

Acta Materialia, 231, 117902 (2022)

View on Google Scholar →
Key Points
  • Systematic DFT study with brute-force configurational analysis of ten TM segregation and co-segregation at ferritic iron grain boundaries
  • Introduced a novel quantitative method for assessing cohesion effects through the DDEC6 bond-order framework
  • Differing GB characters change not only TM segregation behaviours but also their effects on cohesion
  • Combined multi-element segregation effects differ substantially from the sum of individual contributions

An understanding of hydrogen embrittlement in Ni grain boundaries from first-principles

Mai, H.L., Cui, X.Y., Scheiber, D., Romaner, L. and Ringer, S.P.

Materials & Design, 212, 110283 (2021)

View on Google Scholar →
Key Points
  • DFT study of hydrogen segregation, accumulation, and cohesion effects at three model Ni grain boundaries: Σ3(111), Σ5(120), and Σ11(110)
  • GB character controls vulnerability to hydrogen embrittlement; low-energy coherent GBs minimise H accumulation
  • Interfacial cohesion diminishes by 6.7–37.5% depending on H accumulation levels
  • Chemical bond-order approach enabled quantitative determination of fracture paths arising from H embrittlement

Mechanical properties of ultrathin gold nanowires from first-principles: Interdependencies between size, morphology and twin boundaries

Mai, H.L., Cui, X.Y. and Ringer, S.P.

Physical Review Materials, 4(8), 086003 (2020)

View on Google Scholar →
Key Points
  • DFT tensile simulations of <111> ultrathin gold nanowires (~1–1.6 nm diameter) as a function of size, morphology (rectangular/hexagonal prism), and ultrahigh-density twin boundaries
  • Remarkable range of Young's modulus (60–158 GPa) and ultimate tensile strengths (3.7–14.0 GPa) explains the scatter observed in experiments as an intrinsic feature
  • Ultrahigh twin boundary density universally enhanced strength across all sizes and morphologies, attributed to suppression of anisotropic phenomena and slip plane disruption

The role of vacancies in electric field mediated graphene oxide reduction

Mai, H.L., Cui, X.Y., Stampfl, C. and Ringer, S.P.

Applied Physics Letters, 113(7), 073103 (2018)

View on Google Scholar →
Key Points
  • DFT study of electric-field-mediated reduction of oxygen and hydroxyl groups from pristine and defective graphene sheets with single and double vacancies
  • Determined critical electric field strengths required for progressive and selective removal of different functional groups
  • Vacancy defects significantly inhibit the effectiveness of electric fields for reducing O and OH groups, due to complex interactions between functional groups and vacancy edge atoms
